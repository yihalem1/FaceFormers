# FaceFormers


Project Overview
In this project, we've innovated in the realm of 3D face reconstruction by replacing the traditional backbone with a transformer model. Our primary focus was to enhance the inference speed without compromising the quality of the reconstruction.

Key Highlights:
Backbone Replacement: Transitioned from the conventional ResNet 50 architecture to a more efficient transformer model.
Performance Metrics: Conducted comprehensive speed measurements, confirming that our transformer-based approach significantly outperforms ResNet 50 in terms of inference speed.
Inference Efficiency: Demonstrated a noticeable improvement in speed, making the process faster and more efficient, ideal for real-time applications.
This repository documents our journey of integrating transformer models into 3D face reconstruction and presents a detailed analysis of the performance gains achieved, particularly in inference speed. We invite collaborators and enthusiasts to explore, experiment, and contribute to this evolving field.
